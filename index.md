I am a "La Caixa" Junior Leader Postdoc in the Conservation Ecology & Society group at CE3C. My research focuses on testing, improving and applying Species Distribution Models (SDMs) to many species at different spatial scales. 
For many years I have been involved in terrestrial ecology research using carnivores as model system, with a special focus on conservation and coexistence. I have previously worked as a postdoc in CZU Prague, the National Museum of Natural Sciences (CSIC) Madrid, and Universidad Autónoma de Madrid. I received my Ph.D. in Ecology from the University of Oviedo in 2020 focused on brown bear ecology and conservation.

**Ecological Niche Modelling in Iberia research group**

In my research group, we explore the effect on species’ distributions of predator-prey interactions to promote conservation and coexistence in human-dominated landscapes. My ongoing project aims to integrate biotic interactions (such as predation and competition) into SDMs for several carnivore species, with the goal of producing more accurate predictions to inform and support effective conservation strategies while also producing more user-friendly frameworks for their optimal application. Our research focuses on the Iberian Peninsula, recognizing Iberia as a coherent biogeographical unit while also strengthening connections between Spanish and Portuguese researchers. 


## 🌍 Research focus
- Species distribution models (SDMs)
- Carnivore ecology and distribution
- Iberian biodiversity integration (Spain + Portugal)
